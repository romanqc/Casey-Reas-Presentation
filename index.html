Casey Reas

Computational Artist & Co-Founder of Processing

Who is Casey Reas?

Casey Reas is an American artist, educator, and co-founder of Processing, the revolutionary programming language and environment that has democratized creative coding for millions worldwide.

His work explores the intersection of software, conceptual art, and data visualization, creating dynamic systems that generate form through algorithmic processes.

Currently a Professor at UCLA's Design Media Arts department, Reas has exhibited internationally at institutions including the Whitney Museum, SFMOMA, and Centre Pompidou.

The Birth of Processing

2001

Foundation

Casey Reas and Ben Fry initiated Processing at MIT Media Lab, building upon concepts from John Maeda's Design By Numbers.

2004

Public Release

Processing 1.0 was released as open-source software, making creative coding accessible to artists, designers, and educators worldwide.

Now

Global Impact

Processing has become one of the most influential tools in creative coding, with a thriving community of millions and derivatives like p5.js expanding its reach.

Processing: Software Sketchbook

Processing was designed to teach fundamentals of computer programming within a visual context and to serve as a software sketchbook.

  • Simplicity: Learn to code through immediate visual feedback
  • Accessibility: Free, open-source, and cross-platform
  • Community: Extensive libraries, tutorials, and global user base
  • Expression: Bridge between code and visual art
